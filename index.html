About the Department

The Department of Library and Information Science at the University of Abuja is a vital academic unit dedicated to training professionals in the management, organization, and dissemination of information. While specific historical details about its founding are not widely published online, the department is part of the broader mission of the University of Abuja, which was established in 1988 to provide high-quality education in Nigeria‚Äôs Federal Capital Territory

The LIS department has grown to reflect the evolving nature of the field, integrating both traditional librarianship and modern information science. It offers undergraduate and postgraduate programs designed to equip students with skills in cataloging, classification, digital librarianship, information retrieval, and knowledge management. The department also emphasizes research, information ethics, and the use of ICT in library services.

Notably, the department benefits from experienced faculty, including scholars like Dr. Victoria Okojie, a prominent figure in Nigerian librarianship and information science. Her involvement has helped shape the department‚Äôs academic and professional standards.

The University of Abuja Library itself serves as a central resource hub, supporting the department‚Äôs academic goals by providing access to both physical and electronic resources, including e-books, journals, and databases.

Since its inception, the department has expanded its academic offerings to meet the growing demand for skilled information professionals.
